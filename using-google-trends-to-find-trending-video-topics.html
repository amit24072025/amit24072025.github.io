What is Google Trends and Why Is It a Creator's Best Friend?

Google Trends is a public web facility by Google that shows how often a particular search term is entered relative to the total search volume over a given period. For video creators, its value is immense. Instead of guessing what content might perform well, you can see tangible data on what your potential audience is actively looking for, allowing you to create content that directly meets current demand.

Step-by-Step Guide to Finding Video Topics

  1. Start at the Source: Navigate to the official Google Trends website.
  2. Explore Your Niche: Use the main search bar to enter a broad topic related to your channel (e.g., "AI art", "vegan recipes", "gaming setup").
  3. Crucial Filter - Target YouTube: This is the most important step. By default, Trends shows "Web Search" data. Click on it and change the filter to "YouTube Search". This ensures the data reflects what people are searching for on the video platform itself. Also, consider adjusting the time frame (e.g., "Past 30 Days" or "Past 90 Days") to find recent trends.
  4. Analyze "Related Queries": Scroll down to the "Related queries" box. You'll find two tabs: "Top" and "Rising". While "Top" shows the most popular queries, "Rising" is your goldmine. It reveals queries that have had the most significant growth in search volume recently. A query marked Breakout is experiencing explosive growth and represents a prime video opportunity.
  5. Compare and Validate: Use the "+ Compare" feature to pit different video ideas against each other. This helps you see which topic has more search momentum on YouTube and prioritize your content production accordingly.

Advantages and Potential Outcomes

  • First-Mover Advantage: By identifying "Rising" topics, you can create content before larger channels, capturing the initial wave of viewers and subscribers.
  • Data-Driven Content Strategy: Move beyond guesswork. Use real-world search data to make informed decisions about your content calendar.
  • Understand Seasonality: Discover if topics are more popular at certain times of the year (e.g., "fitness goals" in January) and plan your content accordingly.
  • Use Case Example: A gaming creator notices a new indie game is marked as "Breakout" in the "Rising" queries. They quickly produce a "first impressions" video, ranking high in YouTube search for that game and gaining thousands of views from an eager, new audience.
